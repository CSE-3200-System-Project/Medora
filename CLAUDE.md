## Design Context

### Users
Medora serves patients, doctors, and admins in Bangladesh healthcare workflows.
Users often interact in high-stakes moments where time, clarity, and trust matter.
Core jobs to be done:
- Patients: onboard quickly, manage medical history, find doctors, book appointments, track prescriptions and reminders.
- Doctors: manage schedule, review patient context safely, run consultations, and create structured prescriptions.
- Admins: verify doctors and maintain platform safety and operations.

### Brand Personality
Voice and tone should feel reliable, safe, and productive.
Primary emotional outcomes are calm, trust, clarity, and warmth, with explicit emphasis on safety, reliability, and efficiency.
The interface should feel like a professional healthcare platform (reference direction: established reliable healthcare products, e.g., ShasthyaSheba-like trust cues) and must never feel like an unfinished student project.

### Aesthetic Direction
Adopt a minimal-clinical, premium-polished, friendly-human, and data-dense professional direction.
Preserve the medical-blue core visual identity and avoid overly bright or clashing colors that do not harmonize with doctor blue.
Support light and dark themes equally.
Use subtle motion only, always with reduced-motion fallbacks.

### Design Principles
1. Safety-First Clarity
Every screen should reduce cognitive load in healthcare-critical tasks using clear hierarchy, plain labels, and explicit states.

2. Trust Through Professional Consistency
Use consistent spacing, typography, and component behavior so the product feels reliable across all flows and roles.

3. Medical-Blue Visual Discipline
Keep the blue-led palette as the anchor; use restrained accents and avoid saturated, mismatched, or novelty-heavy color combinations.

4. Mobile-First, Role-Aware UX
Prioritize mobile ergonomics and responsive layout while preserving role-specific workflows (patient, doctor, admin) without visual fragmentation.

5. Inclusive by Default
Design to WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline, maintain strong contrast and touch targets, and ensure all interactions remain usable with reduced motion enabled.
