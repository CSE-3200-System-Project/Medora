## Design Context

### Users
Medora serves three primary user groups in Bangladesh: patients managing personal health data, doctors running day-to-day clinical workflows, and administrators overseeing verification, safety, and operations. Most authenticated usage is task-driven and time-sensitive, where users need to scan, decide, and act quickly with minimal cognitive overhead.

### Brand Personality
Calm, trustworthy, and clinical. The interface should communicate safety-first healthcare delivery and operational confidence. Tone should feel professional and serious, with clear hierarchy and low ambiguity instead of flashy or playful presentation.

### Aesthetic Direction
Data-dense but readable healthcare UI with strong structure, left-to-right scanning clarity, and compact information grouping. Medora uses a medical-blue visual foundation and emphasizes practical decision support over decorative card repetition. Visual rhythm should come from spacing hierarchy and information architecture, not visual noise.

### Design Principles
1. Decision-first hierarchy: prioritize what needs action now, then context, then deep detail.
2. Structured clarity: prefer grouped, labeled, and scannable data over repeated decorative blocks.
3. Clinical restraint: keep visual language calm, consistent, and trustworthy.
4. Progressive disclosure: expose deeper diagnostics only when requested via expandable detail layers.
5. Responsive density: preserve information richness on desktop while maintaining legibility and touch safety on mobile.
